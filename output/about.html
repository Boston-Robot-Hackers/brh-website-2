Boston Robot Hackers

CONNECT * LEARN * BUILD

We are a vibrant community of robotics enthusiasts, engineers, students, and makers who come together to explore the fascinating world of robotics.

Our Mission

Boston Robot Hackers is a community-driven organization dedicated to fostering innovation, learning, and collaboration in the field of robotics. We bring together enthusiasts, professionals, students, and makers who share a passion for building intelligent machines and pushing the boundaries of what's possible with robotics.

What We Do

Monthly Meetups: We gather regularly to share knowledge, showcase projects, and learn from each other. Our meetings feature presentations, hands-on workshops, and collaborative problem-solving sessions.

Collaborative Projects: From autonomous delivery bots to sensor networks, our members work together on exciting robotics projects that combine cutting-edge technology with practical applications.

Learning & Growth: Whether you're a complete beginner or an experienced engineer, we provide a supportive environment for continuous learning. Our community includes experts in ROS, computer vision, mechanical design, and embedded systems.

Open Source: We believe in the power of open source collaboration. Many of our projects are shared publicly to benefit the broader robotics community.

Our Community

We welcome members from all backgrounds and experience levels:

  • Students exploring robotics for the first time
  • Professional engineers working in the field
  • Makers and hobbyists with a passion for building
  • Researchers developing new techniques and algorithms
  • Entrepreneurs bringing robotic solutions to market

Meeting Location

Our meetings take place at Artisans Asylum in Allston, Massachusetts - a perfect maker space environment that provides access to tools, equipment, and the collaborative atmosphere that makes our community thrive.

Get Involved

Ready to join our community? Here's how you can get started:

  1. Attend a meeting - Check our events page for upcoming gatherings
  2. Join our online discussions - Connect with members between meetings
  3. Start or join a project - Collaborate on something amazing
  4. Share your knowledge - Give a presentation or lead a workshop

The future of robotics is being built by communities like ours. Come be part of it!
